Commodities: Decoupling from the Market & Institutional Dark-Pool Flow

SHOPPA research note · data as of 2026-06-12 · universe: 44 commodity-related tickers

What this is

A data view of the commodity complex answering two questions: (1) which commodities are decoupling from the broad stock market (S&P 500 / SPY) — the kind of break that defined the 1970s commodity era — and (2) where institutional money is quietly accumulating or distributing through dark pools. It is a regime / context view, not a price forecast.

Data source

All figures are computed from Massive.com (formerly Polygon.io) market data, Developer tier (15-minute delayed; the delay does not affect daily figures):

  • Regime metrics (Beta, Corr, CorrΔ, RS): from daily closing prices, roughly the trailing 13 months, for each ticker and for SPY.
  • Dark-pool flow: from the individual trade tape over the last 5 sessions, keeping only off-exchange (dark-pool / FINRA TRF) prints.

How each metric is defined

BetaSensitivity to the S&P 500 — covariance of the ticker's daily returns with SPY's, divided by SPY's variance (~13 months). 2.0 means it tends to move about twice as much as SPY; a negative beta means it tends to move opposite the market.
CorrHow tightly it currently tracks SPY — the correlation of daily returns over the last 30 days, from −1 (opposite) through 0 (independent) to +1 (lockstep).
CorrΔ
(decoupling)		The change in that 30-day correlation versus ~60–90 days earlier. Negative = decoupling (the link to the market is weakening); positive = re-coupling.
RS 60d
(relative strength)		The ticker's total return over the last 60 trading days minus SPY's over the same period. Positive = outperforming the market.
Dark net
(5 sessions)		Net institutional dark-pool flow: the sum of (direction × dollar size) across off-exchange prints of at least $50,000, excluding mechanical hedge / contingent / average-price prints. Positive = net accumulation (buying); negative = distribution. $0 means no qualifying prints were found (sparse data, not an absence of activity).
FlagA combined read. The headline pattern is DECOUPLE + LEAD + ACCUM — a commodity simultaneously breaking from the market, outperforming it, and being accumulated. That is the bullish "1970s-style" combination.

Current findings

The energy complex is the group genuinely decoupling

Energy names show low-to-negative beta and falling correlation to SPY — they are breaking away from the broad market: XLE, XOP, XOM, CVX, AR, KMI, DVN, PBR, OXY, RRC, EQT, HAL. However, most are decoupling downward (negative relative strength) — independence, not yet leadership.

Materials & miners are high-beta amplifiers, still coupled

Steel, copper and diversified miners carry high beta (≈1.3–2.6) with correlation to SPY that is high and rising, while strongly outperforming: CLF, MT, FSLR, TECK, ENPH, FCX. These move with the market, amplified — which also means they would amplify a market decline.

Where institutions are accumulating (dark pools, last 5 sessions)

Largest net dark-pool accumulation: COPX, TAN, XLE, ALB, DVN, BHP, NUE, AR, PSLV, PBR.

The bullish "1970s" combination

Names currently decoupling AND leading AND being accumulated: (none). This pattern is not present in the universe right now — energy is breaking away but falling, while the leaders remain tied to the market.

Methodology & honest caveats

Dark-pool direction is an estimate. Dark prints hit the tape without a buyer/seller flag, so buy-vs-sell is inferred with the tick rule (uptick = buy, downtick = sell) — noisy for midpoint prints. A pending upgrade to real-time quote data would replace this with proper (Lee-Ready) classification. Until then, treat accumulation/distribution as a directional hint.
These metrics are descriptive, not predictive. Beta, correlation and relative strength measure what has happened over a finite window and they shift over time. Separate rigorous testing found that dark-pool flow did not predict future price in a way that survives trading costs — so this is a tool for informing discretionary decisions and identifying regime, not an automated trading signal.
